This workshop explored the intersection of game and AI as a novel way to approach architectural participatory generative design. The aim was to establish workflows for tool-based exploration and experiment in game design environment through Unity3D and its package ML-agents. Explorations into the extensive potential of AI and its interaction through framed problems were understaken and discussed. We further explored novel generative design methodology driven by Reinforcement Learning combined with player active interaction, focusing on the actor-aware generation of logics and complex spatial perceptions. QUestions like 'How can a machine think creatively', 'What may be the possible form generated from the active collaboration between AI and players?' and' What are the machine-made elements that become unperceivable from a human?', approach the goals of AI proponents concerning the new generation of AI. This would help designers through the novel augmentation of machine vision and automation. As architects and designers, allowing a wider spectrum of evaluation will be vital in the design process.


I developed a game for the final project in this workshop which uses objects from the fantasy world of Harry Potter as agents, like staircases, key figure-heads, sword etc. This game was developed in a very short span of 24 hours, therefore is experimental at this stage. The agents used Reinforcement Learning technique to actively multiply and respond to the human-machine interaction, as trained.
